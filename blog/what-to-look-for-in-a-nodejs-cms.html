What to look for in a Node.js CMS

What to look for in a Node.js CMS

When a web developer and designer wants to be able to handle a large amount of work without wasting time or fixing bugs and issues, they know that they need a reliable Content Management System in order to get the job done.

Contrary to popular belief, a good Node.js CMS is not something that is meant to be only for inexperienced coders. It’s also meant to be for the seasoned designer that wants to make their job easier and wants to get more work done when they dealing with deadlines.

With that said, it’s very important to be able to choose the right CMS for your needs. Today, we are going to give you some of the most important details on the features you want to see in your CMS. This will be essential for your design and development needs.

The top features you want to see in a Node.js Content Management System

A simple and painless installation process

By simple, we mean that within a couple of steps you should have your CMS ready to go, and by painless we mean that it shouldn’t be plagued with bugs and issues that are going to set your project back.

A powerful content editor

Some CMS have very weak content editions features. This means that they have a small number of features to tweak text, images, tables, and any other content related modifications to help you enhance the look of any website. The CMS you choose to use should provide a powerful content editor, or at least the option to enhance it with ease.

Third party integrations

This is very important because it will add an extra dimension to the CMS when the community contributes to it’s growth. The more third party support that a CMS can get, the more likely there will be a solution for any design and development roadblock that you can face during a project.

Ecommerce features

No CMS can be complete today if there is no Ecommerce support, being able to setup a shop in order to sell products with ease is an essential part of any CMS structure.

A large support community

The amount of support that is given to a CMS is going to be very important. The larger the community, the more likely you are to find a solution to any problem that you have by asking in forums and groups.

Solid core functionality

This means that it should be easy for the CMS to maintain stability when you make use of the features it offers. A good development foundation for a CMS goes a long way once a large number of people tests it.

These are a few Node.js CMS options that you will find very reliable:

  • SonicJs
  • Strapi
  • Cody
  • Ghost
  • NodeBeats

Final thoughts

There are many CMS options out there and you will find some that may not be as good as you expect.
